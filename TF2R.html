Titanfall 2

Why Titanfall 2 is the FPS you should be playing 2 years later...

April 13, 2018

Since the release of the original 1993 DOOM, the First-Person Shooter genre has become a ubiquitous element of popular video games. Yearly entries in multiple series of First-Person Shooters by AAA MegaDevelopers are outright expected in the modern market of games. Players by the millions grind for hours each day in these shooters’ online modes, seeking everything from scoreboard glory to a coveted weapon or item. Considering the recent, explosive popularity of Battle Royale style online shooters, as well as the enduring presence of the FPS genre as a whole, one could have difficulty trying to find the best overall experience in a supersaturated market of often mediocre games. However, one needs only to look back to October 28, 2016 and the release of Respawn Entertainment’s Titanfall 2 to find all the elements of a great shooter and more. Titanfall 2, even two years later, is still arguably the most unique, well polished, challenging, and fun FPS available to all platforms and FPS enthusiasts. While similar to most modern first person shooters, Titanfall 2 sets itself apart by directly addressing and solving many problems that bring down otherwise good shooter games. Not only does Titanfall 2 solve problems, it builds on already strong elements such as movement mechanics, level design, and both PVE and PVP play. Simply, Titanfall 2 is the shooter that delivers the best experience for new and veteran shooter fans alike, and it is the best overall choice for shooter fans, even two years later.

Today, most FPS games are heavily multiplayer focused. Games such as Blizzard’s Overwatch (2016) and, in fact, the original Titanfall (2014) are played exclusively online with and/or against other players. Many consumers who focus solely on multiplayer often skip FPS games’ single player campaigns entirely, possibly contributing to the often less-than- stellar state of some recent popular shooter campaigns. After the lackluster, or outright lack of, a single player campaign in the original Titanfall, Respawn Entertainment responded to this criticism by adding a notably well-crafted single player campaign (by shooter standards) to Titanfall 2. Throughout the campaign, a player is given the opportunity to try and experiment with all Pilot and Titan weapons and loadouts, as well as fight against all types of Titans, thus preparing them for online play and allowing them to settle into a play style that works for them. While deep moral conflicts and revelations may be absent from the narrative of Titanfall 2’s campaign, watching the main character be thrust into action with his new Titan, and forming a fraternal bond with the machine over the course of the campaign is surprisingly charming. Notable also is the quality and originality of level design in Titanfall 2’s campaign. Sequences spanning vast canyons with bottomless chasms, navigating the constantly moving platforms of a massive industrial facility, and jumping from present to past and back again in time to cross gaps are all memorable instances that push a player and the game’s wall running movement mechanics to their limits in Titanfall 2’s stellar single player campaign.

As with most conventional, popular FPS games today, online multiplayer is a principle component of the overall experience. Players will likely spend far more time in this game mode than in any offline single-player campaign. Ttianfall 2 is no exception, offering a multiplayer mode that far surpasses any of its competitors, both recent and past. The Titanfall experience was built on multiplayer, the first game being played exclusively online in a multiplayer setting. However, Titanfall 2’s multiplayer not only expands upon the successes of the previous iteration, improving the experience, but it improves upon the online FPS experience overall, mitigating or solving many of the problems or quirks that arise in traditional online shooters. By design, Titanfall discourages camping and other static, trap-like tactics that can frustrate other players and devalue the experience. Players are simply too fast, and too well-equipped to counter such tactics, that their use puts a practitioner at a disadvantage in such an environment as Titanfall 2’s. The expansion of customization and player choice is expanded well in Ttianfall 2, after fans of the first game expressed that there were too few customization options present. The abundance of different ability and weapon options for Pilots as well as a more than doubled roster of Titans diverse in their kits, opens a new world of choices in an already stellar environment for players to discover and develop new play styles that fit their desires. The Titans themselves are as joyous and tactful to use as they are unique, a contrast to passive or uninvolved kill streak rewards in other shooters. Titanfall 2’s online multiplayer is ultimately the highest and most polished evolution of any multiplayer game of its type, delivering blistering speed, strategic gameplay options, outstanding level design, and hours of intense fun for players seeking an online experience.

Creating and sustaining games in the modern market has become a huge task for even the largest of AAA developers. The need for servers and maintenance of those servers, as well as the intervention of developers when a certain game mechanic is broken or unfavorable to all players is common especially in massively popular online shooter games. Developers must also preform an increasingly difficult balancing act when it comes to pleasing their fans, creating a good game, and monetizing said game to be profitable. Titanfall 2’s developer, Respawn Entertainment, juggles all these responsibilities exceptionally well due in part to lessons learned with the first Titanfall but also the past experiences of the core development team, most of whom were developers for the studio Infinity Ward working on the massively popular FPS Call of Duty: Modern Warfare series. Using this past experience in creating quality FPS games, Respawn created the first Titanfall, which was met with criticism for being too barebones, having too few customization options, and lacking in a dedicated single player campaign. Respawn then did something much bigger developers often have difficulty doing: they listened to the feedback and criticism and resolved to improve, then they actually followed through on this. In their second effort, Respawn created a respectable single-player campaign, a fleshed-out and improved multiplayer mode with many customization options, and even went so far as to rework and improve the matchmaking system after it was shown to be lacking post-launch. While these steps are unquestionably commendable, and are more than other developers would do to deliver a better experience, the concern of how the game would be monetized is an increasingly valid concern among game enthusiasts. Predatory business practices by large developers including progression-stunting mechanics to encourage (or force) the purchase of microtransactions/ RNG (Random Number Generator) loot boxes are becoming alarmingly common in popular online games. However, Respawn Entertainment has taken steps to ensure that no invasive microtranscations taint the online experience of Titanfall 2, promising that allnon-cosmetic post-launch content will be free to all players. So far, Respwan has kept this promise, delivering consistent, if a bit sparse, free content and updates throughout the post- launch life of Titanfall 2. When criticized by players early on for not being transparent enough with their free content update plans, Respawn agains took steps to cultivate goodwill and trust from their player base by releasing a month-to- month roadmap of all the free content updates for Titanfall 2. Though there are microtransactions present in Titanfall 2, the content to which they are tied is purely cosmetic, and does not effect gameplay in any way. This is likely the most ethical way to include microtransactions in a game like this, as one can simply ignore them completely if they do not wish to buy them, and enjoy the game to its fullest potential while doing so. If one is, however, interested in purchasing these microtranscations, they feature no alternate currency or RNG of any kind. See a skin pack you like? Simply buy it, no need to gamble and hope that one receives the item they desire. All things considered, Respawn Entertainment stands as a beacon in an increasingly darkening or apathetic industry, showing that it is possible to make a good game, include monetization without alienating players, and properly support said game today, even doing so as a studio a fraction of the size of AAA developers that cannot do these same things.

In the hyper-competitive game market of the modern era, with games of nearly endless variety originating from all corners of the world, it is rare to find any single game that checks all the boxes of goodness. Innovation, accessibility, replay value, proper support, and fun factor all rarely come in one package today. Ttianfall 2, however, is one such package. It is a complete, modern shooter that satisfies and even improves upon all that which a dedicated shooter fan could ask for. A stellar campaign that is enjoyable for new players or shooter veterans that prepares one for PVP, an outstanding multiplayer mode that truly innovates and remedies the problems of typical online multiplayer shooters, and a dedicated, friendly, talented development studio that actually listens and puts players over profits are all reasons why Titanfall 2 is the shooter that you should be playing two years later.
